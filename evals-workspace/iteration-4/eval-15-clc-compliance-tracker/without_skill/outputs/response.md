# Client Money Obligations Under CLC Regulation

As a licensed conveyancer regulated by the Council of Licensed Conveyancers (CLC), you have significant obligations around client money. These are set out primarily in the CLC's **Accounts Code** (part of the CLC Handbook) and reinforced by the **Code of Conduct** principles.

## Core Principles

The CLC operates an **outcomes-focused** regulatory framework. The overarching outcomes relevant to client money include:

- **Acting with integrity** and in the best interests of your clients
- **Keeping client money safe** — this is a fundamental obligation
- **Maintaining public trust** in the profession and the regulatory system

## Key Obligations

### 1. Separate Client Account

- You **must** hold client money in a designated **client account** that is separate from your office/business account.
- The client account must be held at a bank or building society approved by the CLC.
- The account must be clearly labelled/designated as a client account.

### 2. Proper Accounting and Record-Keeping

- You must maintain **proper books of account** that record all client money transactions accurately and promptly.
- Records must show the position on each individual client matter at any time.
- You must be able to produce a full list of client ledger balances on demand.
- All transactions must be recorded within the required timeframes (typically promptly, and no later than the next working day).

### 3. Reconciliation

- You must carry out **regular reconciliations** of client account — typically at least every five weeks (monthly plus a buffer).
- Reconciliations must compare the client account bank balance with the total of individual client ledger balances and the client account cash book balance.
- Any discrepancies must be investigated and resolved promptly.

### 4. No Borrowing or Personal Use

- Client money must **never** be used for your own purposes or for the purposes of the practice.
- You must not borrow from the client account.
- Office money must be transferred out of the client account promptly.

### 5. Prompt Handling

- Client money must be paid into the client account **promptly** — usually on the day of receipt or the next working day.
- Money due to the client must be returned promptly when there is no longer a reason to hold it.
- Residual balances must not be left sitting indefinitely; you must take reasonable steps to return money to clients.

### 6. Interest

- You have obligations regarding **interest on client money**. Where client money is held for a material period or in a material amount, you must account to the client for interest earned (or a fair equivalent).
- You should have a written policy on when interest will be paid.

### 7. Client Money Protection (Insurance/Compensation)

- The CLC requires practices to have appropriate **insurance arrangements** in place.
- The CLC operates a **Compensation Fund** which provides a safety net for clients who suffer loss due to dishonesty or failure to account for client money.
- Practices contribute to this fund through their annual fees.

### 8. Accountant's Report

- You must deliver an **Accountant's Report** to the CLC annually (or at the intervals specified by the CLC).
- This report must be prepared by a qualified accountant who examines your compliance with the Accounts Code.
- Qualified accountants must report any significant breaches they find.

### 9. Third-Party Managed Accounts (TPMAs)

- The CLC has provisions for the use of **Third-Party Managed Accounts** as an alternative to traditional client accounts.
- If you use a TPMA, you must still ensure client money is protected, but the detailed accounting obligations may differ.
- You must ensure the TPMA provider is appropriate and that clients are informed.

### 10. Supervision and Training

- If you manage or supervise staff who handle client money, you must ensure they are **properly trained and supervised**.
- The practice must have adequate **systems and controls** in place to safeguard client money.

## CLC Enforcement

The CLC takes client money breaches very seriously. Consequences of non-compliance can include:

- **Disciplinary action** — warnings, fines, conditions on your licence
- **Suspension or revocation** of your CLC licence
- **Intervention** into the practice — the CLC has powers to intervene and take control of client files and money where there is a risk to clients
- **Criminal prosecution** in cases of dishonesty

## Practical Steps for Compliance

1. **Have clear written procedures** for handling client money within your practice.
2. **Train all staff** who handle or have access to client money.
3. **Reconcile regularly** and investigate discrepancies immediately.
4. **Keep office and client money strictly separate** — when in doubt, treat money as client money.
5. **Return client money promptly** when the retainer ends or the matter completes.
6. **Prepare for your annual accountant's report** by maintaining clean records throughout the year.
7. **Report breaches** to the CLC promptly if they occur — proactive disclosure is viewed more favourably than concealment.

## Important Distinction: CLC vs SRA

Note that CLC-regulated firms follow the **CLC Accounts Code**, not the SRA Accounts Rules. While there are similarities, the specific requirements differ in detail. CLC firms should always refer to the CLC Handbook rather than SRA guidance.
