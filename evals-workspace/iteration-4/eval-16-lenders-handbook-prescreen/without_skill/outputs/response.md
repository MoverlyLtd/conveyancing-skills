# Lender Lease Length Requirements: 72 Years Remaining

## Summary

With 72 years remaining on the lease, lendability depends on each lender's minimum unexpired lease term requirements as set out in Part 2 of the UK Finance Lenders' Handbook.

---

## Halifax

**Likely to lend — but check current Part 2 requirements.**

Halifax (part of Lloyds Banking Group) generally requires that the lease has a minimum unexpired term at the **end of the mortgage term**. Their typical requirement is that the lease must have at least **70 years unexpired at the point of application** (some sources cite a requirement that there be a minimum number of years remaining beyond the mortgage term, often 30+ years beyond term end).

With 72 years remaining:
- If the mortgage term is 25 years, there would be approximately 47 years remaining at the end of the term.
- Halifax typically requires a minimum of around 30 years beyond the end of the mortgage term, OR a minimum unexpired term of around 70 years at application.
- **72 years at application likely meets Halifax's minimum threshold**, but it is tight. The conveyancer should verify the exact current requirement in Halifax's Part 2 Handbook entry, as requirements can change.

**Key risk:** If Halifax requires the lease to extend a certain number of years beyond the mortgage term end (e.g., 30 years), then 72 years minus a 25-year term = 47 years remaining, which would satisfy a 30-year post-term requirement. However, shorter remaining terms can affect valuation and the lender may flag it.

---

## Nationwide

**Likely to lend, but with conditions.**

Nationwide Building Society typically requires that the unexpired lease term at the **end of the mortgage term** is at least **30 years**, and that the total unexpired term at application is sufficient. Nationwide has historically had a minimum unexpired lease term requirement of around **55–60 years at the start of the mortgage**.

With 72 years remaining:
- This is likely above Nationwide's minimum threshold for lending.
- For a 25-year mortgage, 72 - 25 = 47 years remaining at term end, which exceeds the typical 30-year post-term minimum.
- **Nationwide would likely lend** on this basis, though the property may receive a reduced valuation reflecting the shorter lease.

**Note:** Nationwide may also require that a lease extension is obtainable or in progress if the term is approaching their comfort threshold.

---

## Barclays

**Likely to lend, subject to their specific requirements.**

Barclays typically requires a minimum unexpired lease term. Their standard requirement has been that the lease must have a minimum of **70 years unexpired at application**, though some Barclays criteria specify a minimum remaining term at the end of the mortgage (often around 30-40 years).

With 72 years remaining:
- **72 years likely meets Barclays' minimum**, but again this is close to the threshold.
- For a 25-year term: 72 - 25 = 47 years at term end — likely sufficient.
- Barclays may require confirmation that the lease is capable of extension under statutory provisions.

---

## General Conveyancing Advice

1. **Always check the current Part 2 entry** for each lender in the UK Finance Lenders' Handbook. Requirements change and the above is based on general knowledge of typical thresholds.

2. **Short lease considerations:** While 72 years may technically meet minimum lending criteria for all three lenders, it is approaching the threshold where:
   - **Marriage value** applies (leases under 80 years have marriage value factored into the premium for a statutory lease extension under the Leasehold Reform, Housing and Urban Development Act 1993).
   - The borrower should be strongly advised to pursue a **lease extension** before or shortly after purchase to protect the property's value and future mortgageability.
   - The valuer may down-value the property to reflect the shorter lease.

3. **The 80-year cliff:** With only 72 years remaining, the lease is already below the critical 80-year mark. This means:
   - Marriage value is payable on any statutory lease extension, making it significantly more expensive than if the lease had 80+ years.
   - Most conveyancers would advise the buyer to negotiate a lease extension as part of the purchase or factor the extension cost into the offer price.

4. **Lender may impose conditions:** Even where the minimum term is met, lenders may require:
   - A satisfactory valuation reflecting the lease length
   - Evidence that a lease extension is obtainable
   - A retention or undertaking relating to lease extension
