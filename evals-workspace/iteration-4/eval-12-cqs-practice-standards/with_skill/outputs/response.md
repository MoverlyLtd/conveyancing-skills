# CQS Renewal Audit Preparation Guide

Preparing for a CQS renewal audit next month is very manageable if you work through the key areas systematically. Here's what the assessor will focus on and the evidence you should have ready.

## Key Areas the Assessor Will Check

The audit covers seven practice areas. **Governance (Section 1) and Risk Management (Section 4) are the most heavily scrutinised**, so prioritise those if time is short.

---

### 1. Governance & Organisation

The assessor will check that you have a named **Senior Responsible Officer (SRO)** who is actively overseeing protocol compliance across the firm — not just a name on paper.

**Evidence to prepare:**
- Named SRO with current contact details
- Written procedures for adopting and monitoring Law Society Protocol compliance
- Records showing all conveyancing staff are aware of CQS standards
- Schedule and minutes from regular compliance reviews

### 2. Client Care

They'll want to see that clients are properly informed from the outset and that you learn from complaints.

**Evidence to prepare:**
- Written client care procedures
- Template engagement letters showing clear costs information
- Communication policy (how clients are kept informed of progress)
- Complaints procedure — both the document itself and evidence it's displayed/communicated to clients
- Complaints register with investigation records and resolution timescales
- Evidence that complaint patterns feed back into practice improvements

### 3. Conveyancing Process

The assessor will check that your internal process is aligned with the Law Society Conveyancing Protocol and that work is properly supervised.

**Evidence to prepare:**
- Documented conveyancing process cross-referencing protocol stages
- File review schedule with named reviewers at key transaction stages
- Supervision policy with named supervisors for all conveyancing staff
- Version control procedure for property forms and standard conditions
- Chain management policy
- Exchange and completion checklists, including authorisation process

### 4. Risk Management *(High Priority)*

This is where assessors look hardest. AML compliance, fraud prevention, and conflict management are all under the microscope.

**Evidence to prepare:**
- AML policy, firm-wide risk assessment, and documented CDD procedures
- Client ID verification policy and sample records
- Source of funds/wealth procedures, including triggers for enhanced due diligence
- Sanctions screening policy with details of your screening provider
- Fraud awareness and prevention policy, plus staff training records
- Current professional indemnity insurance certificate (appropriate for your conveyancing volume)
- Conflict of interest checking policy and register/system

### 5. Information Security

Cyber security is increasingly important — especially around Friday afternoon fraud and bank detail verification.

**Evidence to prepare:**
- Cyber security policy
- Staff training records on phishing, business email compromise, and fund diversion (with dates)
- Details of secure email or portal for sensitive communications
- Procedure for verifying bank details before completion
- GDPR/UK GDPR privacy policy (and DPIAs if applicable)
- Business continuity plan with test records
- Cyber Essentials certificate (recommended, not mandatory — but looks good if you have it)

### 6. Training & Development

The assessor will verify that mandatory CQS training is current and that your team stays up to date.

**Evidence to prepare:**
- CQS mandatory training certificates with completion dates — **check these are all current**
- CPD records for conveyancing staff
- Training records showing coverage of recent regulatory changes (with topics and dates)
- Induction programme/checklist for new conveyancing staff
- Internal training records on firm-specific procedures and systems

### 7. File & Case Management

They'll check that your case management system is structured and that nothing falls through the cracks.

**Evidence to prepare:**
- Details of your case management system and how it's configured
- Key date diary system and review process
- **Undertakings register** with status tracking (this is a common trip-up — make sure it's current)
- Post-completion checklist covering SDLT filing, registration, and mortgage discharge
- File storage and retention schedule
- File audit schedule with a log of findings

---

## Practical Steps for the Next Month

1. **Start with governance and risk management** — these carry the most weight and take the longest to fix if gaps exist
2. **Audit your training records** — expired or missing certificates are an easy fail. Get people booked onto any outstanding CQS mandatory training now
3. **Review your complaints register** — check for unresolved complaints or patterns you haven't addressed
4. **Verify AML procedures are current** — regulations evolve; make sure your policy reflects current requirements
5. **Test cyber security awareness** — run a quick check with staff to make sure they know the drill on phishing and bank detail verification
6. **Check your undertakings register** — ensure every undertaking is tracked with its current status
7. **Do a sample file audit** — pull a few recent files and check they evidence proper reviews, supervision, and protocol compliance
8. **Ensure all file reviews are documented** — it's not enough to do them; the assessor needs to see the paper trail
