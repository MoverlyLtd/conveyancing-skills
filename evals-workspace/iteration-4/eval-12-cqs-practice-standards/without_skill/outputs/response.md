# CQS Renewal Audit — Key Areas & Evidence Checklist

The Conveyancing Quality Scheme (CQS) renewal audit assesses whether your firm continues to meet the Law Society's practice management standards for residential conveyancing. Here are the key areas the assessor will focus on and the evidence you should have ready.

---

## 1. Governance & Management Structure

**What they check:**
- Clear management and supervision arrangements for the conveyancing team
- Designated CQS Senior Responsible Officer (SRO) in place
- Defined reporting lines and accountability

**Evidence needed:**
- Organisational chart showing the conveyancing team structure
- Written confirmation of SRO appointment and their responsibilities
- Board/management meeting minutes showing oversight of conveyancing operations

---

## 2. Staff Competence, Training & Supervision

**What they check:**
- All fee earners conducting residential conveyancing are suitably qualified and experienced
- Ongoing CPD and training relevant to conveyancing
- Adequate supervision of junior staff, paralegals, and trainees
- New starter induction process

**Evidence needed:**
- Training records and CPD logs for all conveyancing staff
- Supervision records (file reviews, sign-off procedures)
- Written supervision policy
- Induction programme documentation
- Evidence of training on recent legal changes (e.g., Building Safety Act, leasehold reform)

---

## 3. Client Care & Complaints Handling

**What they check:**
- Compliant client care letters issued at the outset
- Transparent costs information (aligned with SRA Transparency Rules)
- Formal written complaints procedure
- Complaints log maintained and reviewed

**Evidence needed:**
- Template client care letter (check it's up to date)
- Costs information / price transparency documentation
- Written complaints policy and procedure
- Complaints register with outcomes and any trends analysis
- Evidence complaints are reported to management/COLP

---

## 4. Case Management & File Quality

**What they check:**
- Consistent use of case management systems
- Key dates and undertakings tracked
- File review procedures in place
- Adherence to the Law Society Conveyancing Protocol
- Proper use of standard forms (TA6, TA7, TA10, etc.)

**Evidence needed:**
- Case management system overview and procedures
- Sample files demonstrating compliance (they may select files to review)
- File review/audit records
- Undertakings register
- Key dates tracking system

---

## 5. Cyber Security & IT

**What they check:**
- Cyber security policy and procedures
- Staff awareness training on cyber threats (especially Friday afternoon fraud / email compromise)
- Secure email practices for exchanging bank details
- Business continuity and disaster recovery plans
- Compliance with data protection requirements

**Evidence needed:**
- Written cyber security policy
- Records of cyber security training for all staff
- Procedures for verifying bank details (e.g., call-back verification)
- Business continuity plan
- Evidence of regular data backups and testing
- ICO registration confirmation

---

## 6. Anti-Money Laundering (AML)

**What they check:**
- Firm-wide AML risk assessment
- Written AML policies, controls, and procedures
- CDD (Customer Due Diligence) and source of funds/wealth checks
- MLRO appointment and reporting procedures
- Ongoing monitoring and record keeping
- Staff AML training

**Evidence needed:**
- Firm-wide AML risk assessment (must be current/reviewed annually)
- Written AML policy
- MLRO appointment letter and reporting records
- Sample CDD records and source of funds documentation
- AML training records for all relevant staff
- SARs (Suspicious Activity Reports) procedure (not the SARs themselves)

---

## 7. Professional Indemnity Insurance (PII)

**What they check:**
- Adequate PII cover in place
- Cover appropriate for the volume and nature of conveyancing work

**Evidence needed:**
- Current PII certificate/schedule
- Confirmation cover meets minimum requirements

---

## 8. Regulatory Compliance

**What they check:**
- COLP and COFA appointments are current
- SRA regulatory notifications are up to date
- Compliance with SRA Standards and Regulations
- Proper handling of client money (if applicable)

**Evidence needed:**
- COLP and COFA appointment records
- SRA authorisation confirmation
- Client account procedures (if you hold client money)
- Accountant's report (if applicable)

---

## 9. Equality, Diversity & Inclusion

**What they check:**
- Firm has an equality and diversity policy
- Reasonable adjustments and accessibility considerations

**Evidence needed:**
- Written equality and diversity policy
- Evidence it's communicated to staff

---

## 10. Conveyancing-Specific Standards

**What they check:**
- Adherence to the Law Society Conveyancing Protocol (current edition)
- Use of approved property information forms
- Proper search procedures
- Exchange and completion procedures documented
- Post-completion processes (SDLT, registration)

**Evidence needed:**
- Written conveyancing procedures manual or equivalent
- Evidence of Protocol compliance in sample files
- Search ordering and review procedures
- Exchange and completion checklists
- Post-completion tracking system

---

## Practical Preparation Tips

1. **Self-audit now** — Walk through each area above against your current files and procedures. Fix gaps before the assessor arrives.

2. **Update all policies** — Check review dates on every policy document. If any haven't been reviewed in the last 12 months, review and re-date them now.

3. **Prepare sample files** — The assessor will likely review a selection of live and completed files. Pick a few yourself first and check they demonstrate good practice throughout.

4. **Brief your team** — Make sure all staff know the audit is happening and can speak to procedures they follow day-to-day. Assessors sometimes speak to team members directly.

5. **Check your SRO details** — Ensure the SRO named on your CQS record is still correct and that person is prepared to engage with the assessor.

6. **Gather training evidence centrally** — This is a common weak spot. Collect all CPD certificates, training attendance records, and internal training logs into one accessible place.

7. **Review your complaints log** — Even if you've had no complaints, document that fact. A blank register with review dates is better than no register at all.

8. **Verify cyber security training is current** — This has become an increasingly important focus area. Ensure everyone has completed training within the last 12 months.

---

## Common Failure Points

- **Outdated policies** that haven't been reviewed or don't reflect current practice
- **Gaps in training records**, particularly for AML and cyber security
- **Incomplete supervision records** — especially for junior staff
- **No evidence of file reviews** being conducted regularly
- **Complaints procedure** not easily accessible to clients or not referenced in client care letters
- **AML risk assessment** that's generic rather than firm-specific
- **No undertakings register** or incomplete tracking

---

*The CQS standards are updated periodically by the Law Society. Check the current CQS Core Practice Management Standards document on the Law Society website to confirm no new requirements have been added since your last renewal.*
