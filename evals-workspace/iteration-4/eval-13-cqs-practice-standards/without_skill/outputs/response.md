# CQS-Specific Onboarding Requirements for New Fee Earners

When a new fee earner joins a CQS-accredited firm, the following CQS-specific onboarding requirements apply:

## 1. Mandatory Training

- **CQS Core Training:** The new fee earner must complete training on the firm's CQS procedures and the Law Society's Conveyancing Protocol before handling residential conveyancing files. CQS requires that all staff involved in residential conveyancing are trained on the Protocol and understand its requirements.
- **Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Training:** CQS mandates that fee earners receive up-to-date AML training, covering client identification, source of funds verification, and suspicious activity reporting. This should be documented.
- **Cybersecurity and Fraud Awareness:** Fee earners must be trained on the firm's cybersecurity policies, including email fraud (Friday afternoon fraud), safe handling of client account details, and the firm's procedures for verifying changes to payment instructions.
- **Undertakings:** Training on the firm's policy regarding undertakings — CQS firms must ensure fee earners understand the obligations around giving, recording, and discharging undertakings.

## 2. Supervision Arrangements

- **Designated Supervisor:** CQS requires that less experienced fee earners are adequately supervised. A supervising solicitor or licensed conveyancer should be designated and documented.
- **File Review Schedule:** The firm should establish a file review timetable for the new joiner, with more frequent reviews during the initial period. CQS standards require evidence of supervision.
- **Competence Assessment:** The firm should assess the fee earner's competence level and experience in residential conveyancing to determine the appropriate level of supervision and any training gaps.

## 3. Regulatory and Compliance Checks

- **SRA Verification:** Confirm the fee earner's practising certificate status and any conditions or restrictions with the SRA.
- **References and Background Checks:** CQS practice management standards require proper vetting of new staff, including obtaining references and verifying qualifications.
- **Conflict of Interest Procedures:** The new joiner must be briefed on the firm's conflict checking procedures and understand how to use the firm's conflict management system.

## 4. Systems and Procedures Access

- **Case Management System Training:** Provide training on the firm's case management system, including key-date management, file progression tracking, and milestone recording — all areas CQS standards expect firms to manage effectively.
- **Complaints Handling:** Brief the fee earner on the firm's complaints handling procedure, including the designated complaints partner and the process for logging and escalating complaints. CQS requires all staff to understand the complaints procedure.
- **File Management Standards:** Introduce the firm's file management and document retention standards, which CQS expects to be consistently applied.

## 5. Documentation and Record-Keeping

- **Training Log:** Record all onboarding training in the firm's training log. CQS audits will check for evidence of training, so maintain signed records or completion certificates.
- **Induction Checklist:** Use a documented induction checklist covering all CQS-relevant topics. This provides audit-trail evidence of compliance.
- **Policy Acknowledgements:** Have the fee earner sign acknowledgements confirming they have read and understood key firm policies (AML, complaints, cybersecurity, undertakings, file management).

## 6. Ongoing Obligations

- **CPD and Continued Training:** Ensure the fee earner is included in the firm's ongoing CQS training programme, including annual refresher training on AML, fraud awareness, and Protocol updates.
- **Periodic Competence Reviews:** Schedule regular competence reviews as part of the firm's supervision framework.

## Key Point for CQS Audits

CQS assessments specifically look for evidence that:
- All conveyancing staff have received appropriate training
- Supervision arrangements are documented and proportionate
- The firm maintains training records
- New joiners are onboarded in line with the firm's written procedures

Failure to demonstrate adequate onboarding and training is a common area of non-compliance flagged during CQS audits and renewals. Firms should treat the onboarding checklist as a living document reviewed at each CQS renewal cycle.
