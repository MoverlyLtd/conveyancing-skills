# CQS-Specific Onboarding Requirements for a New Fee Earner

When a new fee earner joins a CQS-accredited firm, several practice standards kick in. Here's what you need to cover:

## 1. Formal Induction Programme (Standard 6.4)

CQS requires an **induction programme for new conveyancing staff**. This should be a documented checklist covering everything the new joiner needs to know before they start handling files. Keep the completed checklist on record — it's evidence you'll need at audit.

## 2. Training on Firm-Specific Procedures and Systems (Standard 6.5)

Beyond general induction, the new fee earner must be trained on your **firm's specific procedures and systems** — your case management system, your file review process, your chain management approach, how you handle exchange and completion. Document what training was delivered and when.

## 3. CQS Standards and Protocol Awareness (Standards 1.4 & 5.8)

All conveyancing staff must be **aware of CQS standards and the Law Society Conveyancing Protocol**. Your new fee earner needs to understand both — what CQS requires of the firm and how the Protocol governs each transaction. Record that this training has been delivered.

## 4. CQS Mandatory Training (Standard 6.1)

CQS requires **mandatory training to be completed on time**. Check what the current Law Society mandatory training modules are and ensure your new joiner completes them within the required timescale. Keep certificates and completion dates on file.

## 5. Cyber Security Training (Standard 5.2)

Staff must be **trained on cyber security risks** — specifically phishing, business email compromise (BEC), and fund diversion fraud. This is especially critical for fee earners who will be handling completions and client money. Record the training with dates.

## 6. Fraud Awareness Training (Standard 4.5)

Separate from cyber security, the firm needs **fraud awareness and prevention measures** in place, with staff trained accordingly. Make sure the new fee earner understands your firm's fraud policy and knows the red flags.

## 7. AML Procedures Training (Standards 4.1–4.4)

The new fee earner must understand your firm's:
- **Anti-money laundering policy** and risk assessment approach
- **Client identification and verification** procedures
- **Source of funds/wealth** procedures and enhanced due diligence triggers
- **Sanctions screening** procedures

These are heavily scrutinised at audit — don't skip this.

## 8. Supervision Arrangements (Standard 3.3)

CQS requires documented **supervision arrangements for all conveyancing staff**, with named supervisors. Assign a supervisor for the new fee earner and document this in your supervision policy before they start handling files.

## 9. Complaints Procedure Awareness (Standard 2.4)

Ensure the new joiner knows your **written complaints procedure** — how complaints are received, logged, investigated, and resolved. They need to know the timescales and who to escalate to.

## 10. Conflict of Interest Procedures (Standard 4.7)

Train the new fee earner on your **conflict checking procedures** — how to run checks, when to escalate, and how your conflict register works.

## 11. Regulatory Change Updates (Standard 6.3)

Bring the new fee earner up to speed on **recent regulatory changes** relevant to conveyancing. Keep a record of topics covered and dates.

## Practical Onboarding Checklist

| Action | CQS Standard | Evidence to Keep |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| Complete induction programme | 6.4 | Signed induction checklist |
| Train on firm's systems & procedures | 6.5 | Internal training record with dates |
| CQS standards & Protocol awareness | 1.4, 5.8 | Training record |
| Complete CQS mandatory training | 6.1 | Certificates, completion dates |
| Cyber security training | 5.2 | Training record with date |
| Fraud awareness training | 4.5 | Training record with date |
| AML/CDD/sanctions training | 4.1–4.4 | Training record with date |
| Assign named supervisor | 3.3 | Updated supervision policy |
| Complaints procedure briefing | 2.4 | Training record |
| Conflict checking training | 4.7 | Training record |
| Regulatory updates briefing | 6.3 | Training record with topics/dates |
| Add to CPD plan | 6.2 | Updated CPD records |

## Key Tips

- **Document everything** — CQS audits look for evidence, not just intent. Every training session needs a date, a topic, and ideally a signature or completion record.
- **Don't wait** — most of this should happen in the first week, before the fee earner picks up live files.
- **Governance is priority** — sections 1 (Governance) and 4 (Risk Management) are the most heavily scrutinised at audit, so AML, fraud, and supervision arrangements should be locked down from day one.
- **Check current requirements** — CQS standards evolve. Verify the latest mandatory training modules and any new requirements at [lawsociety.org.uk](https://www.lawsociety.org.uk/topics/property/conveyancing-quality-scheme).
