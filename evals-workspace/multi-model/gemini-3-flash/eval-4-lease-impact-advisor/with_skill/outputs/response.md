### 2. Marriage Value & Extension Costs
Because the lease is below 80 years, **Marriage Value applies**. Under the Leasehold Reform Act 1993, the leaseholder must pay the freeholder 50% of the "profit" (the increase in the property's value) generated by the extension.

*   **Impact:** This makes the extension significantly more expensive than if it were 81 years. 
*   **Legislative Note:** The *Leasehold and Freehold Reform Act 2024* is now law and intends to abolish marriage value, but this part of the Act is **not yet in force**. You must advise the vendor based on current law, where marriage value remains a major cost.

### 3. Saleability and Pricing Impact
At 68 years, you should expect a price discount of **15–30%** compared to a similar property with a long lease. Buyers who are aware of the cost of extension and the difficulty of refinancing will factor this into their offers. 

If marketed at £425,000 without an extension in progress, you may find that only cash buyers engage, who will typically look for a "short lease discount."

### 4. Recommended Strategy for the Vendor
To maximize the sale price and open the buyer pool to standard mortgage applicants, we suggest the vendor considers the following options (to be discussed with a specialist lease extension solicitor):

*   **Assign the Benefit of a Section 42 Claim:** This is the most common solution for a mid-transaction sale. The vendor starts the formal statutory process (which adds 90 years and reduces ground rent to a peppercorn) and then "assigns the benefit" of that notice to the buyer on completion. This allows the buyer to bypass the normal 2-year ownership requirement to extend.
*   **Informal/Voluntary Negotiation:** The vendor can approach the freeholder directly to negotiate a new lease. This is often faster but can result in less favorable terms (like higher ground rent) which might still put off some lenders.
*   **Future Value Protection:** Even if a buyer is found who accepts the 68-year term, they should be advised that the lease will continue to depreciate. Extending now is always cheaper than extending later as the term continues to drop.

**Recommendation:** Before marketing at £425,000, the vendor should ideally obtain a formal lease extension valuation to understand the exact cost of the marriage value. This allows you to market the property either "with the benefit of a lease extension" or at a price that accurately reflects the upcoming cost to the buyer.
