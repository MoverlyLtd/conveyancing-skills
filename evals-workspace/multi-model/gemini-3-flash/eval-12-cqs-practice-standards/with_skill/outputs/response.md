To prepare for your Conveyancing Quality Scheme (CQS) renewal audit, you must demonstrate that your firm’s systems and processes consistently meet the Law Society’s Core Practice Management Standards (CPMS).

It is vital to distinguish between **CQS firm-level requirements** and **individual SRA competence requirements**. CQS accredits the firm’s collective systems, procedures, and adherence to the Conveyancing Protocol. In contrast, the SRA regulates the individual competence and professional conduct of your solicitors separately.

The assessor will focus on the following key areas and evidence:

### 1. Governance and Supervision
The assessor will check that your firm has a clear management structure for conveyancing.
*   **Senior Responsible Officer (SRO):** You must have a named SRO responsible for CQS compliance.
*   **Supervision:** CQS requires documented supervision procedures at the firm level (e.g., how often files are checked and by whom). This is distinct from the **SRA Code of Conduct**, which sets the standards for individual supervision of staff.
*   **Evidence:** Updated organization chart, SRO appointment records, and documented supervision policies signed by supervisors.

### 2. Risk Management and AML
This is often the most heavily scrutinized area. You must show that your firm proactively manages transaction risks.
*   **AML & Fraud:** You need robust procedures for Anti-Money Laundering (AML), Source of Funds (SOF), and Source of Wealth (SOW).
*   **Conflict Checks:** Documented evidence of how you check for conflicts of interest at the start of every matter.
*   **Evidence:** Your firm-wide Risk Assessment, AML policy, and specific examples of SOF/SOW checks within files.

### 3. Training and Development
The assessor will verify that all relevant staff are up to date with their learning.
*   **CQS Mandatory Training:** You must show that all staff have completed the mandatory Law Society CQS training modules and Protocol compliance training. 
*   **SRA CPD vs. CQS Training:** Note that while the SRA requires individual solicitors to maintain their own **Continuing Professional Development (CPD)** records to ensure ongoing competence, CQS specifically requires firm-wide records of protocol and procedure training.
*   **Evidence:** A central training register, induction checklists for new staff, and CQS training certificates.

### 4. Information Security and Cyber Crime
With the rise in property fraud, your firm’s digital hygiene is a priority.
*   **Bank Detail Verification:** You must have a written procedure for how you verify client bank details before transferring funds, particularly on completion.
*   **Staff Awareness:** Staff must be trained on phishing, Business Email Compromise (BEC), and fund diversion.
*   **Evidence:** Your Cyber Security Policy, Business Continuity Plan, and records of staff cyber-awareness sessions. (Holding a **Cyber Essentials** certificate is highly recommended).

### 5. File Management and Audits
The assessor will look for consistency across your files to ensure the Law Society Conveyancing Protocol is being followed.
*   **File Reviews:** You must demonstrate regular, documented internal file audits. These should check for protocol compliance and adherence to your firm's own procedures.
*   **Undertakings:** You must maintain a centralized register of all undertakings given and received.
*   **Evidence:** File review logs/findings, the undertakings register, and post-completion checklists (covering SDLT and Land Registry requirements).

### 6. Client Care and Complaints
The firm must show it learns from mistakes.
*   **Complaints Handling:** You must have a written procedure and a central register of complaints. 
*   **Learning from Complaints:** You must show evidence that complaints are reviewed and that any necessary changes to firm procedures have been implemented as a result.
*   **Evidence:** Complaints register, template engagement letters with clear cost information, and minutes of management meetings where complaints were discussed.

### Preparation Checklist for Next Month:
1.  **Audit your Training:** Ensure no CQS mandatory training modules have expired.
2.  **Verify the Protocol:** Ensure all staff are using the most current Law Society property forms and standard conditions.
3.  **Check File Reviews:** Ensure your file review schedule is up to date and that any "actions required" from previous reviews were completed.
4.  **Test Cyber Awareness:** Run a quick briefing or test to ensure staff know the procedure if they receive a request to change bank details.

*Note: CQS is a Law Society scheme available only to SRA-regulated firms. Requirements evolve, so you should verify the latest standards on the Law Society website before your audit.*
