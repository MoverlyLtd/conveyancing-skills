To understand the rules governing restrictive covenants on freehold property, you should consult several authoritative legal sources. In England and Wales, the treatment of restrictive covenants depends heavily on when they were created and how they are registered.

### 1. Key Legal Distinctions
According to the **Law Society**, a restrictive covenant is a private agreement in a deed where one party promises another not to do certain things with their land. (Source: [https://www.lawsociety.org.uk/topics/property/restrictive-covenants](https://www.lawsociety.org.uk/topics/property/restrictive-covenants))

The rules differ based on the date the covenant was created:

*   **Pre-1926 Covenants:** These are governed by common law and equity. For a restrictive covenant to be enforceable against subsequent owners, it must be shown that the covenant "touches and concerns" the land, as established in the landmark case of *Tulk v Moxhay*.
*   **Post-1925 Covenants:** These are governed by the **Law of Property Act 1925 (LPA 1925)**. Specifically, **Section 78** of the Act provides for the automatic annexation of the benefit of the covenant to the land, a principle clarified in the case of *Federated Homes v Mill Lodge*.

### 2. HM Land Registry (HMLR) Guidance
When dealing with technical land registration and the evidence required for covenants, you should refer to the **HMLR Practice Guides**:

*   **HMLR Practice Guide 62 (PG62):** While primarily regarding powers of attorney, this guide is relevant when considering the execution of deeds for the modification or release of covenants. (Source: [https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/powers-of-attorney-and-registered-land/practice-guide-62-powers-of-attorney-and-registered-land](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/powers-of-attorney-and-registered-land/practice-guide-62-powers-of-attorney-and-registered-land))
*   **HMLR Practice Guide 19:** This provides details on how notices and restrictions relating to covenants are entered into the register. (Source: [https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/restrictions/practice-guide-19-notices-restrictions-and-the-protection-of-third-party-interests-in-the-register](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/restrictions/practice-guide-19-notices-restrictions-and-the-protection-of-third-party-interests-in-the-register))

### 3. Modification and Discharge
If you are seeking to modify or discharge a restrictive covenant that is no longer deemed relevant or is obstructing reasonable use of the land, the relevant legislation is **Section 84 of the Law of Property Act 1925**.

The specific forum for these applications is the **Upper Tribunal (Lands Chamber)**. They have the power to discharge or modify covenants under specific grounds, such as the covenant becoming obsolete or providing no practical benefit.

### 4. Law Commission Recommendations
For a comprehensive understanding of the proposed reforms and the complexities of current law, refer to the **Law Commission report**, "Making Land Work: Easements, Covenants and Profits à Prendre" (HC 1067). This report highlights the difficulties in the current system of "encumbrances" and suggests modernising the law to replace restrictive covenants with "Land Obligations."
