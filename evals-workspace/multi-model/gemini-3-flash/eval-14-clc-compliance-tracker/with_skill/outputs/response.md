As a CLC-regulated firm, your handling of complaints is governed by the **CLC Code of Conduct (revised effective 1 January 2025)** and the CLC’s outcomes-focused regulatory framework. 

It is important to note that the **CLC Code of Conduct prevails** in all instances where subsidiary codes might appear to conflict. Furthermore, the CLC's requirements for handling complaints differ significantly from those of the SRA or the Law Society; CLC firms operate under an outcomes-focused model rather than a prescriptive protocol.

### 1. Core Requirement: Ethical Principle 3
Under **Ethical Principle 3 (Act in the best interests of clients)**, Outcome 3.6 specifically requires that you handle complaints fairly and promptly. Because the CLC is an outcomes-focused regulator, your firm has the flexibility to develop its own internal procedures to meet this outcome, provided they are effective.

### 2. Regulatory Compliance Manager (RCM) Oversight
Unlike SRA-regulated firms which use COLP/COFA roles, your firm’s **Regulatory Compliance Manager** is responsible for ensuring that the complaints process complies with CLC standards and that any systemic issues (like recurring delays) are addressed at a firm-wide level.

### 3. Required Actions for a Delay Complaint
When a complaint regarding delays is received, the CLC requires the following:

*   **Acknowledge and Investigate:** You must acknowledge the complaint promptly and investigate it fairly in accordance with the written procedures provided to the client in your **Terms of Engagement (Outcome 3.2)**.
*   **Provide a Substantive Response:** You must provide a final response within 8 weeks. If the matter is not resolved to the client's satisfaction within this timeframe, you must inform them in writing of their right to refer the matter to the **Legal Ombudsman (LeO)**.
*   **Documentation and Evidence:** To demonstrate compliance during a CLC audit, you must maintain:
    *   A **Complaints Register** documenting all received complaints.
    *   A **Resolution Timeline** showing when the complaint was received, acknowledged, and resolved.
    *   Evidence of any remedial action taken to address the delay (e.g., file notes or communication logs).

### 4. Comparison with SRA/Law Society
It is vital to remember that:
*   CLC firms **do not use the Law Society Protocol** or follow SRA-specific Complaint Handling Rules.
*   The CLC does not require "CQS" accreditation to prove service quality; your adherence to the CLC Code of Conduct is the benchmark for the market.
*   While an SRA firm might focus on prescriptive steps, the CLC expects you to evidence that the *outcome* (a fair and prompt resolution) was achieved.

### 5. Financial Implications (CLC Accounts Code)
If the delay has resulted in a dispute over interest or the handling of client funds, you must also ensure compliance with the **CLC Accounts Code** (a subsidiary code). This requires that client money is protected at all times and interest is handled according to the terms of engagement agreed upon at instruction.
