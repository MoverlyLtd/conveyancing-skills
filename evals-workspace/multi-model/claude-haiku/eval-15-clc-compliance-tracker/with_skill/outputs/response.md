As a licensed conveyancer under CLC regulation, your key obligations around client money are:

1. Accounts Code Compliance:
   - Client money must be held in a designated client account, separate from the firm's own money.
   - The client account must be reconciled regularly, at least monthly.
   - Interest earned on client money must be handled as per the terms of engagement with the client.
   - An annual accountant's report must be submitted to the CLC.

2. Principle 3 (Act in the Best Interests of Clients):
   - Outcome 3.4 - You must protect client money and assets.
   - This includes having robust systems and controls in place to safeguard client funds.

3. Anti-Money Laundering Requirements:
   - You must verify the source of funds before accepting any client money, to mitigate money laundering risks.
   - Enhanced due diligence is required for high-risk clients or transactions.
   - All suspicious activity must be reported to the National Crime Agency.

4. Transparency:
   - Under the Estimates & Terms of Engagement code, you must provide clients with a clear estimate of costs and disbursements at the outset.
   - You must inform clients promptly of any changes to costs.

The CLC Code of Conduct and subsidiary codes like the Accounts Code take precedence over any conflicting SRA or Law Society equivalents. As a CLC-regulated firm, you must comply with the CLC's outcomes-focused regulatory framework, rather than the more prescriptive SRA requirements.
