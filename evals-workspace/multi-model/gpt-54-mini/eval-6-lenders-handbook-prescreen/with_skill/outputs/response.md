Under the UK Finance Mortgage Lenders’ Handbook, you need to look at **two separate issues**: the **lease term** and the **building regulations issue**.

## 1) Lease term: 72 years remaining
This is a **Part 1 / Part 2** issue under **HB 5.14.1**.

Specific minimum terms vary by lender, so you must check **Part 2** for the actual lender. As a guide for major lenders:

- **Halifax**: **YES** — 72 years exceeds their **70-year** minimum
- **Barclays**: **YES** — 72 years exceeds their **70-year** minimum
- **Santander**: **YES** — 72 years exceeds their **55-year** minimum
- **Nationwide**: **YES** — 72 years exceeds their **55-year** minimum

So on lease length alone, **72 years is acceptable for those lenders**.  
Source: **Part 1, section 5.14.1**; lender-specific requirements are in **Part 2**.

## 2) Loft conversion without building regulations sign-off
This falls under **HB 5.5.1–5.5.2** (planning/building regulations).

The Handbook says you must take all reasonable steps to ensure necessary **planning consents and building regulations approval** are in place. If there is a breach or missing approval, the key questions are:

- Is there a **reasonable prospect of enforcement**?
- Can you still **certify good and marketable title**?

Important timing points:
- The usual **enforcement period for building regulations work is 4 years**
- The usual **time bar for other planning breaches is 10 years**

If the loft conversion is older than the relevant enforcement period, that may reduce the risk. If not, it is generally a matter to **report to the lender** before completion.

A possible resolution is **indemnity insurance** for the building regulations issue, but whether that is acceptable depends on the lender’s **Part 2** requirements.

## Practical answer
- **Lease term (72 years):** likely **acceptable** for major lenders like Halifax, Barclays, Santander, and Nationwide, but check the specific lender’s **Part 2**.
- **Loft conversion without building regs sign-off:** this is a **potential title issue** that may need **reporting to the lender** unless you can satisfy yourself there is no enforcement risk or arrange **indemnity insurance** acceptable to the lender.
