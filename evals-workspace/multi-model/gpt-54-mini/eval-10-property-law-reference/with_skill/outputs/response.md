For restrictive covenants on a freehold property, the main places to look are:

1. **The title register and title plan from HM Land Registry**
   - The title register often mentions restrictive covenants in the **charges register** or may refer to a deed containing them.
   - Start here: HM Land Registry service and guidance:  
     https://www.gov.uk/government/organisations/land-registry

2. **The actual deed or conveyance referred to in the title**
   - The wording of the covenant is usually in an old transfer, conveyance, or deed, not fully set out on the register.
   - If the register refers to a deed, you may need an official copy of that deed/document.

3. **HM Land Registry Practice Guide 62**
   - This is the key HMLR guide to powers of attorney and registered land, and it is relevant if any covenant-related application is being made by an attorney or a document needs to be signed via power of attorney.  
   - https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/powers-of-attorney-and-registered-land/practice-guide-62-powers-of-attorney-and-registered-land

4. **Law Society guidance on restrictive covenants**
   - Useful plain-English overview:  
   https://www.lawsociety.org.uk/topics/property/restrictive-covenants

5. **Law Commission work on restrictive covenants**
   - For background on reform and how the law works in practice:  
   https://lawcom.gov.uk/project/restrictive-covenants/

A couple of important legal points to be aware of:
- Restrictive covenants can differ depending on whether they were created **before 1 January 1926** or **after** that date.
- **Pre-1926 covenants** are generally analysed under common law/equity principles, including whether they “touch and concern” the land, as in *Tulk v Moxhay*.
- **Post-1925 covenants** are affected by the Land Registration/Law of Property Act framework, including automatic annexation issues under **s.78 LPA 1925**, discussed in cases like *Federated Homes v Mill Lodge*.

If you want to change or remove a restrictive covenant, the main statutory route is usually **section 84 of the Law of Property Act 1925**, and applications are made to the **Upper Tribunal (Lands Chamber)**.
