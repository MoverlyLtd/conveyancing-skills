Under **CA Protocol 4.0 (Planning Permission)**, a rear extension built in **2021** with **no planning permission record** should be treated as follows:

- Because the work appears to be **less than 4 years old**, it is **not yet outside planning enforcement risk** under the Protocol’s planning tree.
- The usual CA Protocol response is to **obtain a regularisation certificate or a bespoke indemnity policy** where appropriate.
- You should **advise both the client and the valuer** about the lack of planning permission record, and the **valuer should confirm the property is structurally sound**.
- Also check whether there were any **planning conditions** that may have applied, and whether any consent was actually granted but not recorded.

Important: the CA Protocol treats **planning permission and building regulations as separate checks**. So for a 2021 extension, you should also separately check **building regulations compliance** under **CA Protocol 4.0 (Building Regulations)**:
- If there is **no building regulations completion certificate**, and the works are **post-1984**, you need to consider the building regs position separately.
- For works **more than 12 months and less than 10 years old**, the Protocol says:
  - **non-structural works**: advise client/lender, check with surveyor, and consider indemnity if there is material enforcement risk;
  - **structural works**: advise client/lender, check with surveyor, and **recommend indemnity at the seller’s cost**.

For completeness, the CA Protocol’s enforcement timeframes are:
- **4 years** for operational development / change of use to a dwelling,
- **10 years** for breach of condition / other change of use,
- **12 months** for listed building enforcement.
