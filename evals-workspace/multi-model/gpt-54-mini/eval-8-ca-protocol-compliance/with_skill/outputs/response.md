Under the CA Protocol 5th Edition, delays from unanswered enquiries are dealt with under **Section 1.0–1.3 (reducing transaction delays)** and **Section 2.0–2.4 (additional enquiries)**.

Key points:

- **Respond promptly** to the other side and work cooperatively to avoid delay.  
  - **CA 1.0** says communication should be active and regular, with electronic communication used first where possible.
- **Raise enquiries early** and keep them limited to title/client/lender matters.  
  - **CA 2.0 and 2.4** encourage enquiries to be focused and raised at the earliest stage.
- If the other side is not responding, the protocol expects **regular updates** and active chasing:
  - **acknowledge correspondence within 2 working days**
  - **respond to enquiries within 5 working days**
  - if a substantive reply is not yet possible, **provide weekly updates**
- Use the **phone** where issues are complex or emails are just circling.  
  - **CA 1.0** specifically prefers telephone contact to resolve difficult points rather than prolonged email exchange.
- Keep the client, the other lawyer, and the estate agent updated regularly.  
  - **CA 1.0** refers to maintaining regular updates and diarised file reviews.
- On the seller side, the protocol expects the seller’s conveyancer to review documents early and resolve likely enquiries before the contract pack goes out.  
  - **CA 1.8 and 1.9**.

If the delay is continuing, the protocol’s **escalation route** is:

1. **Raise it with the fee earner** handling the file  
2. If unresolved, **escalate to a senior partner/compliance lead**  
3. If there is still persistent non-compliance, **consider referral to the relevant regulatory body**

- request a **weekly progress update** if they still cannot answer
- update your client and agent
- diarise follow-up and escalate internally if needed
