- **Price impact**: expect a meaningful discount versus an equivalent flat with a longer lease, often in the **15–30%+** range depending on location, ground rent, and other lease terms.
- **Extending before sale is strongly advisable** if the owner wants the widest buyer pool and best price.

Also important: if the buyer needs a **25-year mortgage**, the lease would be only **43 years at the end of term**, which will be a problem for many lenders even if the current term is just about acceptable.

### Marriage value
Because the lease is **below 80 years**, **marriage value applies** under current law. That can significantly increase the cost of extending the lease.

### Extension options to discuss with the seller
1. **Statutory lease extension**
   - Requires the seller to have owned the flat for **2 years**
   - Adds **90 years**
   - Reduces ground rent to a **peppercorn**

2. **Informal/voluntary extension**
   - Can sometimes be done faster
   - Terms are negotiable
   - But it may preserve ground rent or include less favourable clauses, so it needs careful review

3. **Assigning the benefit of a Section 42 claim to the buyer**
   - If the seller qualifies, they can start the statutory process and assign the benefit on completion
   - This can make the flat more marketable without waiting the full 2 years
