For a freehold property, the key places to look for **what restrictive covenants apply** and **how they can be enforced/changed** are:

## 1) Your title documents (to see the actual covenants)
- **HM Land Registry title register** (often has a “Charges Register” section summarising covenants and referring to the deed that contains the full wording).
- **The deed/transfer referred to in the register** (that’s usually where the covenant text sits in full).

Land Registry’s technical guide on understanding **registered titles and what’s in the register** is here:  
> HMLR Practice Guide 2 (Title plans and boundaries) explains how Land Registry titles/plans work at a practical level. (Source: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/title-plans-and-boundaries/practice-guide-2-title-plans-and-boundaries)

And Land Registry’s general practice guide collection (useful to find the right guide quickly):  
> HMLR Land Registration Practice Guides collection. (Source: https://www.gov.uk/government/collections/land-registration-practice-guides)

## 2) A plain-English overview of restrictive covenants (what they are, typical issues)
The Law Society has a consumer-facing overview you can use as a starting point:  
> Law Society guidance on restrictive covenants (what they are and issues that arise in conveyancing). (Source: https://www.lawsociety.org.uk/topics/property/restrictive-covenants)

## 3) Understanding “which law applies” (older vs newer covenants)
When reading about enforceability, it matters when the covenant was created:

- **Pre‑1926 restrictive covenants** are largely governed by **common law/equity principles** and typically require the benefit/burden rules to be satisfied (commonly discussed via the “touch and concern the land” concept and the equitable enforcement line of cases such as *Tulk v Moxhay*).
- **Post‑1925 restrictive covenants** are commonly analysed under the **Law of Property Act 1925** framework (often discussed with **s.78** and “automatic annexation”, associated with *Federated Homes v Mill Lodge*).

(Those are legal “signposts” to help you navigate commentary—your conveyancer will usually apply them to your specific deed/title wording.)

## 4) If you’re asking “can it be removed or changed?”
The usual statutory route people read about is **s.84 Law of Property Act 1925**, where an application can be made to the **Upper Tribunal (Lands Chamber)** to **discharge or modify** a restrictive covenant.

Also useful background:
- **Law Commission work on reform of restrictive covenants** (good for understanding policy, common problems, and reform direction):  
  > Law Commission – Restrictive Covenants project. (Source: https://lawcom.gov.uk/project/restrictive-covenants/)

And if the issue involves someone signing documents via an attorney (this often comes up in transactions/documentary requirements), Land Registry’s power of attorney practice guide is relevant:  
> HMLR Practice Guide 62 (Powers of attorney and registered land). (Source: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/powers-of-attorney-and-registered-land/practice-guide-62-powers-of-attorney-and-registered-land)

## 5) Practical tip: what to request/search
Ask your conveyancer (or if you’re gathering documents yourself) for:
- Official copy of the **title register** and **title plan**
- Copies of **any deed** referred to in the register that “contains restrictive covenants” (often an old transfer)
- Any **building scheme/estate rentcharge/management documents** if it’s an estate development (common source of covenants)
