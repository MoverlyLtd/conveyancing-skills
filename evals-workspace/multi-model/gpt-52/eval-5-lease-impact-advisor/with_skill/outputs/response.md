## Should you still think about a lease extension?
Even though it’s “safe” today, **a lease still depreciates** as it runs down, so an extension can be worthwhile for **future value protection**—especially if the owner plans to hold it long-term.

Key point: **at 95 years, no marriage value applies** (marriage value only kicks in **below 80 years** under the Leasehold Reform Act 1993), so extending now is typically cheaper than waiting until it drops under 80.

## Extension options to be aware of (even if not needed right now)
1. **Statutory Section 42 route** (if the seller has owned 2+ years): adds **90 years** and reduces ground rent to a **peppercorn**.
2. **Informal/voluntary deal** with the freeholder: can be quicker but terms vary (watch ground rent clauses).
3. **Assigning the benefit of a Section 42 claim**: seller starts the statutory process and assigns it to the buyer on completion (useful when a lease is short; less relevant at 95 years).

## When would you actually be concerned?
Not about the **term** at 95 years. The bigger practical risks are usually:
- **Ground rent escalation** (e.g., doubling clauses) affecting lender appetite
- Service charge / major works / cladding (Building Safety Act issues)
- Defective lease terms (repair, insurance, access, etc.)
