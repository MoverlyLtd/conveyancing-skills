As a licensed conveyancer under CLC regulation, your client money obligations are governed by the **CLC Accounts Code** (a subsidiary code under the CLC Code of Conduct). Here are your key obligations:

## Core Client Money Requirements

### 1. **Designated Client Account** (ACC1)
- All client money must be held in a designated client account
- This account must be clearly identified as a client account
- Must be separate from your firm's business account

### 2. **Regular Reconciliation** (ACC2)
- Client accounts must be reconciled regularly
- **Minimum frequency: monthly**
- Reconciliations must identify any discrepancies immediately

### 3. **Interest Handling** (ACC3)
- Interest on client money must be handled according to your terms of engagement
- You must specify in your client care letter how interest will be dealt with
- This applies when client funds are held for extended periods

### 4. **Annual Accountant's Report** (ACC4)
- Must submit an accountant's report to the CLC annually
- This verifies compliance with the Accounts Code
- Required for all CLC-regulated firms handling client money

## Ethical Principles Connection

Your client money obligations also connect to **Principle 3 (Act in Best Interests of Clients)**:
- **Outcome 3.4**: Protect client money and assets
- **Outcome 3.2**: Provide clear information about how client money will be handled (in your terms of engagement)

## CLC vs SRA Distinction

**Important**: CLC-regulated firms follow the **CLC Accounts Code**, not the SRA Accounts Rules. While both cover client money protection, the specific requirements and terminology differ between the regulators.

## Transaction-Level Evidence

To demonstrate compliance, maintain records of:
- Client account bank statements and reconciliations
- Clear terms of engagement explaining interest arrangements
- Annual accountant's reports submitted to CLC
- Any client money movements properly documented

## Hierarchy Rule

Remember: where the CLC Accounts Code conflicts with the main Code of Conduct, **the Code of Conduct prevails** - this is CLC's regulatory hierarchy.

The CLC's **outcomes-focused approach** means you have flexibility in your procedures, but you must deliver these specific outcomes regarding client money protection.
