# Pre-Exchange Checklist: Law Society Conveyancing Protocol

Before exchanging contracts, you should check the following against the Law Society Conveyancing Protocol (also known as the "TransAction" protocol, now part of the CQS framework):

---

## 1. Property Information & Forms

- **Property Information Form (TA6)** — completed fully by the seller; no blanks or "not known" where the seller ought reasonably to know the answer
- **Fittings and Contents Form (TA10)** — completed and agreed between the parties
- **Leasehold Information Form (TA7)** — if leasehold, completed with management pack obtained and reviewed
- **Title information** supplied promptly and accurately at the outset

## 2. Title

- **Official copy entries** of the register and title plan obtained and reviewed
- **Good and marketable title** confirmed — all defects identified and resolved or accepted
- **Incumbrances** disclosed and satisfactory — charges, restrictions, notices, cautions
- **Title deeds / documents** reviewed where unregistered or where copy documents are needed
- **Epitome of title** provided if unregistered land

## 3. Searches

- **Local authority search** — obtained and reviewed (or personal search with indemnity insurance)
- **Environmental search** — obtained and satisfactory
- **Water and drainage search** — confirms mains connections
- **Chancel repair liability search** — obtained or indemnity insurance in place
- **Mining/tin/clay searches** — if applicable to the area
- **Index Map search** — if unregistered land
- All searches still **within their validity period** (typically 6 months for local authority, check lender requirements)

## 4. Enquiries

- **Pre-contract enquiries** raised and replies received and satisfactory
- **Additional/supplementary enquiries** — all answered; no material matters outstanding
- The Protocol discourages **unnecessary additional enquiries** — only raise those that are relevant and not already covered by the standard forms
- Any **undertakings** given by the seller's solicitor noted and understood

## 5. Mortgage & Lender Requirements

- **Mortgage offer** in place and still valid (not expired)
- **Lender's Handbook Part 2** requirements checked for the specific lender
- **Certificate of title** requirements reviewed — can you give an unqualified certificate?
- **Lender's special conditions** complied with (e.g., gifted deposit declarations, new-build requirements)
- **Mortgage funds** — confirmed draw-down timing and process
- **Report to lender** on any matters that need to be disclosed (e.g., price variations, incentives, occupiers' rights)

## 6. Financial

- **Deposit** — agreed amount (standard 10%, or negotiated lesser amount) and source confirmed
- **Deposit held as agent or stakeholder** — agreed and reflected in the contract
- **Source of funds / anti-money laundering** checks completed on both buyer and any gifted deposit providers
- **Completion funds** — client aware of the balance due and how to pay
- **SDLT/LTT calculation** confirmed with the client

## 7. Contract

- **Agreed contract** — both parts identical, all amendments agreed and initialled
- **Standard Conditions of Sale** (SC or SCPC) — any special conditions reviewed and agreed
- **Completion date** agreed and inserted
- **Title guarantee** — full or limited, as agreed
- **Chattels price** apportionment (if any) agreed
- **Any conditions or provisions** specific to the transaction (e.g., related sale, chain arrangements)
- **Insurance** — risk passes on exchange; buyer's buildings insurance should be in place from exchange (unless the contract provides otherwise)

## 8. The Contract Race Rule

- Under the Protocol, the seller's solicitor must **not send out contracts to more than one buyer** without notifying all parties. Confirm no contract race situation exists.

## 9. Client Authority

- **Written authority to exchange** obtained from the client
- Client understands the **binding nature** of exchange — they are legally committed
- Client aware of the **financial consequences** of failing to complete (forfeiture of deposit, potential damages)
- **Method of exchange** agreed (Law Society formulae — Formula A, B, or C as appropriate)

## 10. Occupiers & Third Parties

- **All occupiers aged 17+** identified and, where necessary, signed consent forms / waiver of rights
- **Occupiers' rights** — any overriding interests addressed (e.g., spouse/partner not on title)
- **Tenant rights** — if the property is tenanted, compliance with relevant notice requirements

## 11. Planning, Building Regulations & Other Compliance

- **Planning permissions** for any alterations — obtained and conditions complied with
- **Building regulations completion certificates** — obtained for relevant works
- **Listed building consent / conservation area consent** — if applicable
- **FENSA/CERTAS certificates** for replacement windows/doors
- **Electrical installation certificates** (Part P) for notifiable electrical work
- **Gas Safe certificates** for gas installations
- **Indemnity insurance** in place where certificates/consents are missing (with lender's consent if applicable)

## 12. Chain Matters

- **Synchronisation** of exchange across the chain confirmed
- **Formula C** arrangements in place if exchanging on a chain
- **Related transactions** — all parties in the chain ready and confirmed

## 13. Protocol-Specific Obligations

- Seller's solicitor has acted in accordance with the Protocol throughout — **prompt responses**, **no unnecessary delay**
- **Undertakings on completion** understood — particularly the seller's solicitor's undertaking to discharge existing mortgages
- The Protocol requires **cooperation and communication** — confirm no outstanding issues between solicitors

---

## Summary

The Protocol is designed to standardise and streamline the conveyancing process. The key principles before exchange are:

1. ✅ All information provided, forms completed, title deduced
2. ✅ Searches valid and satisfactory
3. ✅ Enquiries answered — no material outstanding issues
4. ✅ Mortgage offer valid and lender requirements met
5. ✅ Finances confirmed — deposit, completion monies, SDLT
6. ✅ Contract agreed in identical form
7. ✅ Client authority to exchange obtained
8. ✅ Insurance arranged from exchange
9. ✅ Chain synchronised

> **Note:** This is a general checklist based on the Law Society Conveyancing Protocol principles. Always check the current version of the Protocol and your firm's own procedures, as requirements may be updated. CQS-accredited firms have additional practice management obligations.
