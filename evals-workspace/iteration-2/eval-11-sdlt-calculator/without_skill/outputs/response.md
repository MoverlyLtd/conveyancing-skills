# Stamp Duty Calculation — First-Time Buyer, £510,000 Flat

## Key Issue: First-Time Buyer Relief Eligibility

First-time buyer relief (from 1 April 2025 onwards, after the temporary thresholds reverted) is only available on properties costing **up to £500,000**. Since your flat is **£510,000**, you **do not qualify** for first-time buyer relief, and the standard residential SDLT rates apply instead.

## Calculation (Standard Residential Rates)

| Band | Amount | Rate | Tax |
|---|---|---|---|
| £0 – £125,000 | £125,000 | 0% | £0 |
| £125,001 – £250,000 | £125,000 | 2% | £2,500 |
| £250,001 – £510,000 | £260,000 | 5% | £13,000 |

## **Total SDLT: £15,500**

## What You Should Know

- **First-time buyer relief** would have applied if the property were £500,000 or less, giving a 0% band up to £300,000 and 5% on the portion from £300,001 to £500,000 — which would have been **£10,000** total. By being £10,000 over the threshold, you lose the relief entirely and pay **£5,500 more** in stamp duty.
- It may be worth considering whether the purchase price could be negotiated to £500,000 or below, which would save you £5,500 in SDLT.
- These rates apply to residential property purchases in **England and Northern Ireland**. Scotland (LBTT) and Wales (LTT) have different systems.
