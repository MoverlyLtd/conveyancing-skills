# Stamp Duty on a £510,000 Property — First-Time Buyer

Unfortunately, **first-time buyer relief doesn't apply** at £510,000. The relief is only available on properties priced at **£500,000 or below** — there's no partial relief, so once you're over that threshold, standard SDLT rates apply to the full amount.

## Your SDLT: **£15,500**

Here's the band-by-band breakdown:

| Band | Rate | Tax |
|------|------|-----|
| £0 – £125,000 | 0% | £0 |
| £125,001 – £250,000 | 2% | £2,500 |
| £250,001 – £510,000 | 5% | £13,000 |
| **Total** | | **£15,500** |

**Effective rate:** 3.03%

## Worth knowing

- **SDLT must be filed and paid within 14 days of completion** — your conveyancer will usually handle this.
- If you could negotiate the price down to £500,000, you'd qualify for first-time buyer relief and pay just **£10,000** (saving £5,500).
- These rates apply in **England and Northern Ireland** only. Scotland (LBTT) and Wales (LTT) have different rates.

*Rates effective from 1 April 2025. Verify at [gov.uk/stamp-duty-land-tax](https://www.gov.uk/stamp-duty-land-tax) if in doubt.*
