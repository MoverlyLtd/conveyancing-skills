# Lease Impact Assessment: 95-Year Leasehold Flat

## Short answer

**No — 95 years is not a concern.** This falls in the **GREEN** risk band. The lease length has no material impact on saleability, mortgage availability, or pricing. Your buyer should have the full range of mainstream lenders available and the property's value is unaffected by lease length at this level.

## Marriage value

No marriage value applies. Marriage value only kicks in when a lease drops below **80 years**, at which point the leaseholder must pay the freeholder 50% of the increase in property value resulting from the extension. At 95 years, this isn't a factor.

This is actually the **most cost-effective time to extend** — while the lease is above 80 years, extension costs are significantly lower because there's no marriage value component.

> **Note:** The Leasehold and Freehold Reform Act 2024 is now law and is set to abolish marriage value entirely, but this provision is **not yet in force**. Until it takes effect, the 80-year threshold remains the critical cost boundary.

## Forward-looking considerations

While 95 years presents no immediate problem, it's worth the buyer understanding the trajectory:

- In **15 years** the lease will be at 80 years — entering **AMBER** territory where some lender restrictions start to bite
- In **16 years** it drops below 80 and **marriage value** applies, making extension significantly more expensive
- If the buyer plans to hold the property long-term, extending during ownership (while still above 80 years) would be prudent and cost-effective

## Extension options (for future reference)

If the buyer decides to extend during their ownership:

1. **Statutory route (Section 42)** — requires 2 years' ownership, adds 90 years to the current lease, ground rent reduced to peppercorn (£0). The freeholder cannot refuse. Typically takes 6–12 months.
2. **Informal negotiation** — no ownership requirement, terms are negotiable, but the freeholder sets the terms and can be less favourable.

Your conveyancer should advise on the specifics of extension timing and strategy based on the individual circumstances of the lease.
