# Stamp Duty (SDLT) — £275,000 Property Purchase

## Standard Purchase

**Total SDLT: £3,750** (effective rate: 1.36%)

| Band | Rate | Tax |
|------|------|-----|
| £0–£125,000 | 0% | £0 |
| £125,001–£250,000 | 2% | £2,500 |
| £250,001–£275,000 | 5% | £1,250 |
| **Total** | | **£3,750** |

## Other Scenarios

Depending on the buyer's circumstances, the SDLT figure can vary significantly:

| Scenario | Total SDLT | Effective Rate |
|----------|-----------|----------------|
| **Standard purchase** | £3,750 | 1.36% |
| **First-time buyer** | £0 | 0.00% |
| **Additional property** (e.g. buy-to-let, second home) | £17,500 | 6.36% |
| **Non-UK resident** | £9,250 | 3.36% |
| **Additional property + non-resident** | £23,000 | 8.36% |

## Key Notes

- **First-time buyers** pay no SDLT at all on a £275,000 purchase — the nil-rate band is £300,000 for eligible first-time buyers.
- **Additional property surcharge** (5%) applies if the buyer already owns another residential property and isn't replacing their main residence. This can be reclaimed if the previous main residence is sold within 36 months.
- **Non-UK resident surcharge** (2%) applies if the buyer has not been present in the UK for 183+ days in the 12 months before completion.
- The SDLT return must be filed and tax paid within **14 days of completion**.
- These rates apply in **England and Northern Ireland** only. Scotland (LBTT) and Wales (LTT) have different rates.
- Rates current as of 1 April 2025.
