# Stamp Duty Land Tax (SDLT) Calculation — £275,000 Property

## Standard Residential Purchase

Using the SDLT rates effective from 1 April 2025 (when the temporary higher thresholds reverted):

| Band | Rate | Taxable Amount | SDLT |
|------|------|---------------|------|
| £0 – £125,000 | 0% | £125,000 | £0 |
| £125,001 – £250,000 | 2% | £125,000 | £2,500 |
| £250,001 – £925,000 | 5% | £25,000 | £1,250 |

**Total SDLT: £3,750** (effective rate: ~1.36%)

---

## First-Time Buyer

First-time buyer relief (from 1 April 2025) provides a 0% rate on the first £300,000, with 5% on the portion between £300,001 and £500,000.

Since £275,000 is below the £300,000 threshold:

**Total SDLT: £0**

---

## Additional Property (Second Home / Buy-to-Let)

From 31 October 2024, the higher rate for additional dwellings increased from 3% to 5%. This surcharge is added on top of the standard rates:

| Band | Standard Rate | Surcharge | Combined Rate | Taxable Amount | SDLT |
|------|--------------|-----------|---------------|---------------|------|
| £0 – £125,000 | 0% | 5% | 5% | £125,000 | £6,250 |
| £125,001 – £250,000 | 2% | 5% | 7% | £125,000 | £8,750 |
| £250,001 – £275,000 | 5% | 5% | 10% | £25,000 | £2,500 |

**Total SDLT: £17,500** (effective rate: ~6.36%)

---

## Non-UK Resident

Non-UK residents pay an additional 2% surcharge on top of the applicable rates:

- **Standard + non-resident surcharge:** £3,750 + £5,500 = **£9,250**
- **Additional property + non-resident surcharge:** £17,500 + £5,500 = **£23,000**

*(The 2% non-resident surcharge applies across all bands.)*

---

## Important Notes

- SDLT applies to property purchases in **England and Northern Ireland** only. Scotland uses LBTT and Wales uses LTT.
- These calculations assume completion on or after 1 April 2025.
- The first-time buyer relief is only available for purchases up to £500,000.
- The additional property surcharge applies when the buyer already owns (or is treated as owning) another residential property.
- Always verify current rates with HMRC or a qualified conveyancer, as rates and thresholds can change.
