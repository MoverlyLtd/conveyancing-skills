# Stamp Duty (SDLT) Calculation — Second Property at £650,000

For a second/additional residential property purchased in England at **£650,000**, the Stamp Duty Land Tax (SDLT) is calculated as follows.

As an additional property, you pay the standard SDLT rates **plus a 5% surcharge** on each band. The surcharge increased from 3% to 5% from 31 October 2024.

## Calculation (using rates effective from 1 April 2025)

| Band | Portion | Standard Rate | Surcharge | Effective Rate | Tax |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| £0 – £125,000 | £125,000 | 0% | 5% | 5% | £6,250 |
| £125,001 – £250,000 | £125,000 | 2% | 5% | 7% | £8,750 |
| £250,001 – £650,000 | £400,000 | 5% | 5% | 10% | £40,000 |

## **Total SDLT: £55,000**

This represents approximately **8.46%** of the purchase price.

## Key Assumptions

- The property is **residential** and located in **England** (or Northern Ireland).
- You already own at least one other residential property, triggering the higher rates for additional dwellings.
- The purchase completes on or after **1 April 2025**, when the temporary higher nil-rate thresholds expired and reverted to £125,000.
- The 5% additional property surcharge applies (increased from 3% on 31 October 2024).

## Notes

- If the property is in **Scotland**, Land and Buildings Transaction Tax (LBTT) applies instead, with different rates and bands.
- If the property is in **Wales**, Land Transaction Tax (LTT) applies instead.
- If you sell your previous main residence within 3 years of purchasing the new one, you may be able to reclaim the surcharge element.
- Non-UK residents pay an additional 2% surcharge on top of the above rates.
