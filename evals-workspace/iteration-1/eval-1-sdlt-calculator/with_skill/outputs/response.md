# Stamp Duty on a £425,000 Property — First-Time Buyer

As a first-time buyer, the Stamp Duty Land Tax (SDLT) on a £425,000 property is **£6,250**.

## Band-by-Band Breakdown

| Band | Rate | Tax |
|------|------|-----|
| £0–£300,000 | 0% | £0 |
| £300,001–£500,000 | 5% | £6,250 |

- **Total SDLT: £6,250**
- **Effective rate: 1.47%**

## What This Means

First-time buyer relief gives you a **£0 rate on the first £300,000**, so you only pay 5% on the portion between £300,001 and £425,000 (i.e. £125,000 × 5% = £6,250).

Without first-time buyer relief, a standard purchase at this price would attract more tax — so the relief saves you money.

## Important Notes

- **Filing deadline:** You must file your SDLT return and pay within **14 days of completion**.
- **Eligibility:** First-time buyer relief applies if you (and anyone you're buying with) have never owned a residential property anywhere in the world.
- **Threshold:** First-time buyer relief is available on properties up to £500,000. Your purchase at £425,000 qualifies.
- **Location:** These rates apply to **England and Northern Ireland** only. Scotland (LBTT) and Wales (LTT) have different rates.
- Rates current as of 1 April 2025 — verify at [gov.uk/stamp-duty-land-tax](https://www.gov.uk/stamp-duty-land-tax) if in doubt.
