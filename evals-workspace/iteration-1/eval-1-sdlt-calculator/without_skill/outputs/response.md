# Stamp Duty Calculation — First-Time Buyer at £425,000

As a first-time buyer purchasing a property for £425,000, here's the Stamp Duty Land Tax (SDLT) calculation:

## First-Time Buyer Relief

First-time buyer relief applies (available on properties up to £625,000).

The current first-time buyer thresholds (from April 2025) are:

- **0%** on the first **£300,000**
- **5%** on the portion from **£300,001 to £500,000**

## Calculation

| Band | Amount | Rate | Tax |
|------|--------|------|-----|
| £0 – £300,000 | £300,000 | 0% | £0 |
| £300,001 – £425,000 | £125,000 | 5% | £6,250 |

## **Total SDLT = £6,250**

## Key Notes

- First-time buyer relief is available if neither you nor anyone you're buying with has ever owned a residential property (anywhere in the world).
- This relief applies in England and Northern Ireland. Scotland has LBTT and Wales has LTT with their own schemes.
- If you were *not* a first-time buyer, the standard rates would apply and you'd pay more.
